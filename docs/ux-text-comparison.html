Summary for the merged product: BridgeClause wins on almost every piece of user-facing copy — the headlines are warmer, the field hints tell real stories, the loading messages create a narrative moment, and the personalisation with first names makes the output feel bespoke. Legal Empathy Bridge contributes two things worth keeping: its dedicated privacy banner component (more explicit and trust-building than BridgeClause's inline mention), and its waiting / session-status screens (which BridgeClause lacks because of its URL-based architecture). The combined product should adopt BridgeClause's voice and copy throughout, import LEB's privacy banner as a standalone component shown before each form submission, and introduce a proper waiting screen in the session flow. The Hebrew copy in LEB needs a full rewrite — it reads as machine-translated and lacks the warmth of the English original.
